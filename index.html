Interactive signal lab

See an image as frequencies

MRI scanners collect spatial-frequency measurements called k-space. Explore the same Fourier idea with an ordinary grayscale image.

Low frequencies

Overall shape and contrast

Near the spectrum centre, slowly changing patterns describe broad structure.

High frequencies

Edges and fine detail

Farther from the centre, rapidly changing patterns sharpen boundaries and texture.

MRI connection

Measurements before pixels

MRI receives complex-valued k-space samples. An inverse Fourier transform turns those measurements into an image. Real MRI also involves coils, pulse sequences, sampling trajectories, noise, and reconstruction corrections.

Educational analogy only — this app processes ordinary images and is not medical software.
